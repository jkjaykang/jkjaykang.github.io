A v e r a

"The Mother of all living things, Avera watches the world with great adoration."

-Scripture of Avera

Avera is the goddess of nature and life. She is responsible for the creation of elves, animals, and plants that inhabited the world before the Hemiad Explosion. She takes the form of an owl when presenting herself, often merged with other of her creations.

After the Hemiad Explosion, Avera left to her own world along with the other deities. She had hoped to bring the elves back to her own world, but the elves declined, having grown fond of Antri. Avera is the most responsive of all deities, often answering prayers of elven priests and worries of any who choose to worship her. She still holds a deep love for the world of Antri, and is rumored to sometimes explore the everchanging nature taking on a form of an elf.

D E U S

"Deus does not resemble our machines, our machinery is a feeble imitation of its infinite efficiency and complexity."

-Scripture of Deus

Deus is the god of order and time. It's form resembles an endless connection of gears and machinery, so complex and intricate that a mortal will be driven mad once exposed to its form.

Deus is the lawmaker of the deities, dictating how each deity can interact with Antri. While some deities attempt to circumvent Deus' laws, none dare to defy him outright. Deus rarely respond or interact with its worshiper, and even when doing so speaks in cryptic noises and unrecognizable static.

H E M I A D

"A god betrayed by his own creation and laid to eternal slumber, yet he smiles."

-"Observation of Hemiad"

Hemiad is the dormant god of compassion and earth. Having lost his powers and conciousness, he now takes the form of a faded stone tablet, with faint depiction of his original form.

Hemiad is the sculptor of the landscape of Antri, and the creator of the dwarves. After being betrayed and attacked by the dwarves, Hemiad exploded, releasing a wave of energy that changed Antri forever. With his last strength, he protected his attackers from the explosion, allowing some of the dwarves to maintain their form. Eternally regretful of their transgression, the dwarves wait for Hemiad to open his eyes, carrying out the message and will of Hemiad with their action and soul.

O R O S

"You dare attempt to trick me, knowing that every treacherous thoughts and lies you have spouted was that of my design?"

-Oros to Paust, the human who challanged him.

Oros is the god of treachery and change. He takes the form of a mess of snakes and tongues, its shape ever-shifting and changing.

Oros is responsible for tricking the dwarves into betraying their creator, sparking the Hemiad explosion. He disdains his own worshippers and instead approaches mortals he considers interesting. He favors mortals with great ambition that seek to bring change in the world, granting them powers and blessings. Fiendishly, he also enjoys tricking mortals into thinking that they have garnered his favors only to drive them to ruins.

C H E C

"The gates to Chec open only once for each mortal, most never make out."

-"On Chec's realm"

Chec is the god of knowledge and experience. He is responsible for gifting Antri with language. He takes a humanoid form with a giant eye replacing where the head would be.

While many choose to worship Chec, he is seldom responsive to prayers. In part due to his own pursuit of catagorizing of all knowledge and experience in Antri, but mostly due to his unwillingness to drive mortals to madness by conversing with them. Unlike any other diety, Chec's realm is visitable by willing mortals. Described as a torrent of information with a calm refuge in its center where Chec resides, most if not all mortals lose their sense of self in the torrent, never coming back to Antri.
