Starry Night

StarryNight was a navigational tool built in 1999 for the site Rhizome as a way to naviagate posts on its email discussion list. Rhizome's email discussion list was a forum for artists working in emergent forms of media. In StarryNight every forum post is represented as a star that can be clicked on to access the post. Stars become brighter everytime a user interacts with them. Posts are assigned key words and posts that share the same key words become connected in a "constellation" of different stars. StarryNight was created by Mark Tribe, Alexander Galloway, and Martin Wattenberg.

About the artists

Mark Tribe

Mark Tribe is an artist and the creator of Rhizome. His work has been exhibited widely from the New Museum in New York City to the Palais de Tokyo in Paris.

Quote from Tribe on StarryNight: I felt it was important that we begin to develop an aesthetic and theoretical vocabulary to discuss and understand these emergent forms of media art. I initially thought of it as a place for discussion, exchange of ideas, and information, but also for presentation, so people could see one anothers work.

Alexander Galloway

Galloway is an author and professor at the Department of Media, Culture, and Communication at NYU. His work deals with media theory, philosphy, contemporary art, film, and digital media.

Martin Wattenberg

Wattenberg is a scientist who is known for his work in data visualization. He is also an artist who uses interactive graphics as a form of art.
